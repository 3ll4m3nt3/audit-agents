ACCESS CONTROL PROCEDURE

PURPOSE

This procedure defines the steps for managing user access to Acme Corp systems and
information assets. It implements the access control requirements set out in the Company
Information Security Policy.

SCOPE

This procedure applies to all IT systems, applications, and data repositories managed by
or on behalf of Acme Corp. It covers the full lifecycle of access rights: provisioning,
modification, review, and revocation.

ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

IT Helpdesk is responsible for executing access provisioning and revocation requests in
the identity management system.

Line Managers are responsible for approving access requests for their direct reports and
initiating revocation upon any change in role or departure.

The Security Team is responsible for defining access control standards, conducting access
reviews, and investigating access-related incidents.

DEFINITIONS

Privileged access: access granted exclusively to members of the IT Helpdesk and Security Team for managing servers and network infrastructure via the IT Service Portal

Least privilege: the practice of limiting user access to only the systems required for their current role

PROCEDURE

PROVISIONING NEW ACCESS

Step 1 - The requesting manager submits an Access Request Form via the IT Service Portal,
specifying the user, system, role, and business justification.

Step 2 - The IT Helpdesk validates the request is complete and routes it for approval.
Requests for privileged access require additional sign-off from the Security Team.

Step 3 - Upon approval, IT Helpdesk creates or modifies the account within one business
day and notifies the manager and user.

Step 4 - The IT Helpdesk records the provisioned access in the Access Rights Register.

MODIFYING EXISTING ACCESS

Step 1 - The manager submits a Change of Access Form for role changes, promotions, or
transfers that require adjustment of access rights.

Step 2 - IT Helpdesk removes access no longer required before granting new access rights,
following the principle of least privilege.

Step 3 - Changes are recorded in the Access Rights Register with the effective date.

REVOKING ACCESS

Step 1 - HR notifies IT Helpdesk of terminations no later than the employee's last day.
For involuntary terminations, immediate revocation is required.

Step 2 - IT Helpdesk disables the account and removes active sessions within two hours
of notification for involuntary terminations, or at end of final working day for voluntary.

Step 3 - IT Helpdesk confirms revocation to HR and the relevant manager in writing.

Step 4 - Physical access badges and tokens are returned and deactivated.

PERIODIC ACCESS REVIEWS

Access rights shall be reviewed quarterly for privileged accounts and annually for all
other accounts. Line managers receive a report of their team's access rights and must
confirm, modify, or revoke each entry within ten business days.

PRIVILEGED ACCESS MANAGEMENT

Privileged accounts (administrator, root, service accounts) shall be managed via the
Privileged Access Management (PAM) system. Shared passwords are prohibited. All
privileged sessions must be logged and retained for a minimum of 12 months.

EXCEPTIONS

Exceptions to this procedure require written approval from the CISO. All exceptions are
time-limited to a maximum of 90 days and must be recorded in the Exceptions Register.
