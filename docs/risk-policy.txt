# Risk Management Policy

## 1 Purpose

This policy defines Acme Corp's approach to identifying, assessing, treating, and
monitoring information security risks. It ensures that risk management is systematic,
consistent, and aligned with the company's strategic objectives and risk appetite.

## 2 Scope

This policy applies to all information assets, business processes, and third-party
relationships that could expose Acme Corp to information security risk.

## Definitions

Risk: a potential negative outcome arising from a specific threat exploiting a vulnerability, assessed in terms of likelihood and financial impact to the organization

Risk appetite: the maximum level of financial loss the organization is prepared to accept before escalating to executive leadership for treatment approval

Vulnerability: a technical weakness in a system, application, or configuration that could be exploited by an attacker to gain unauthorized access

## 3 Risk Appetite

Acme Corp has a low tolerance for risks that could result in:

- Unauthorized disclosure of customer or employee personal data
- Disruption to critical business services exceeding four hours
- Regulatory fines or legal liability
- Significant reputational damage

Risks scored High or Critical must be treated and reduced to Medium or below before
acceptance. Residual risks rated Medium require CISO approval. Residual risks rated
High or Critical require CEO approval.

## 4 Risk Assessment

### 4.1 Frequency

A formal risk assessment shall be conducted annually. Additional assessments shall be
triggered by significant changes to systems, processes, or the threat landscape.

### 4.2 Methodology

Risk assessments shall use a qualitative approach with a 5x5 likelihood-impact matrix.
Likelihood and impact are each scored 1 (Very Low) to 5 (Very High). Risk score equals
likelihood multiplied by impact.

| Score   | Rating   |
|---------|----------|
| 1 - 4   | Low      |
| 5 - 9   | Medium   |
| 10 - 16 | High     |
| 17 - 25 | Critical |

### 4.3 Asset Inventory

All in-scope information assets shall be catalogued in the Asset Register prior to risk
assessment. Asset owners are responsible for keeping their asset records current.

### 4.4 Threat and Vulnerability Identification

The assessment shall identify relevant threats (e.g. malware, insider threat, natural
disaster) and vulnerabilities (e.g. unpatched software, weak access controls) for each
asset group.

## 5 Risk Treatment

### 5.1 Treatment Options

For each identified risk, the asset owner shall select one of the following treatment options:

- **Mitigate** — implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact
- **Transfer** — share risk via insurance or contractual arrangements
- **Avoid** — cease the activity that generates the risk
- **Accept** — formally accept the residual risk within appetite

### 5.2 Risk Treatment Plan

Accepted treatment decisions shall be captured in a Risk Treatment Plan, which documents
the selected option, planned controls, responsible owner, and target completion date.

### 5.3 Implementation and Tracking

The Security Team tracks open risk treatment items monthly. Overdue items are escalated
to the relevant manager. Items overdue by more than 30 days are escalated to the CISO.

## 6 Risk Monitoring and Reporting

The risk register shall be reviewed quarterly by the Security Team and presented to the
Security Steering Committee bi-annually. Key risk indicators shall be monitored continuously
where tooling permits.

## 7 Roles and Responsibilities

The CISO owns this policy and is responsible for maintaining the risk register, overseeing
risk assessments, and reporting to executive leadership.

Asset Owners are responsible for identifying risks to their assets, selecting treatment
options, and implementing agreed controls on time.

The Security Team facilitates risk assessments, maintains the risk register, and provides
guidance to asset owners.
