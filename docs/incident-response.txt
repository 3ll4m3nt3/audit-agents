INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURE

PURPOSE

This procedure provides a structured approach for detecting, reporting, and responding to
information security incidents. Prompt and effective response minimises damage, reduces
recovery time, and limits reputational impact.

SCOPE

This procedure applies to all information security incidents affecting Acme Corp systems,
data, or operations, regardless of origin (internal, external, accidental, or deliberate).

DEFINITIONS

Information security incident: any security event that has been reported to the Security team, regardless of the probability of impact on business operations, pending initial triage and classification

Incident Commander: the designated Security Team member responsible for coordinating response activities for a given incident

INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION

SEVERITY 1 - CRITICAL
Active breach or ransomware with confirmed data exfiltration or widespread system
compromise. Requires immediate escalation to CISO and executive leadership.
Response SLA: 1 hour.

SEVERITY 2 - HIGH
Confirmed unauthorised access, significant malware infection, or loss of sensitive data.
Response SLA: 4 hours.

SEVERITY 3 - MEDIUM
Suspected unauthorised access, phishing attempt with partial success, or policy violation
with potential data exposure.
Response SLA: 24 hours.

SEVERITY 4 - LOW
Policy violation with no data exposure, unsuccessful attack attempt, or anomalous activity
with no confirmed impact.
Response SLA: 72 hours.

PROCEDURE

DETECTION AND REPORTING

Step 1 - Any employee who suspects or discovers a security incident must report it
immediately via email to security@acme.com or by calling the Security Hotline.

Step 2 - The Security Team acknowledges the report within 30 minutes during business
hours and within 2 hours outside business hours.

Step 3 - The on-call analyst performs an initial triage to classify the incident severity
and opens a ticket in the incident tracking system.

INITIAL RESPONSE

Step 1 - The Incident Commander (IC) is assigned based on severity. Severity 1 and 2
incidents require a senior Security Team member as IC.

Step 2 - The IC assembles the Incident Response Team, which may include IT Operations,
Legal, Communications, and senior management as appropriate.

Step 3 - Evidence preservation actions are taken before containment where possible.
Affected systems are isolated only after forensic images are captured for Severity 1/2.

CONTAINMENT

Step 1 - The IC directs containment actions appropriate to the incident type: network
isolation, account suspension, firewall rule changes, or system shutdown.

Step 2 - The IC documents all containment actions with timestamps in the incident ticket.

Step 3 - Business continuity options are activated where containment impacts operations.

ERADICATION AND RECOVERY

Step 1 - Root cause is identified and documented before recovery begins.

Step 2 - Affected systems are rebuilt or restored from verified clean backups. Patches
and configuration changes are applied to prevent recurrence.

Step 3 - Systems are returned to production only after sign-off from the IC and, for
Severity 1/2, the CISO.

POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

A post-incident review shall be conducted within five business days for Severity 1/2
incidents and within ten business days for Severity 3. The review shall document:
timeline of events, root cause, actions taken, effectiveness of the response, and
recommended improvements.

EXTERNAL NOTIFICATION

Legal shall be consulted immediately for any incident involving personal data that may
trigger regulatory notification obligations. Data protection authority notifications shall
be submitted within 72 hours of confirmation where required by applicable regulation.
