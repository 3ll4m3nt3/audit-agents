# Company Information Security Policy

## 1 Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and
availability of Acme Corp's information assets. It applies to all employees, contractors,
and third parties who access company systems or data.

## 2 Scope

This policy covers all information assets owned, leased, or managed by Acme Corp,
including but not limited to:

- Digital data stored on company systems, cloud platforms, or removable media
- Physical documents containing sensitive or confidential information
- Network infrastructure, endpoints, and communication systems
- Third-party systems that process or store company data

## Definitions

Information security incident: any event that has resulted in, or has the potential to result in, unauthorized access, disruption, disclosure, or misuse of information assets

Privileged access: access rights that allow a user to perform functions beyond those of a standard user, including system administration, configuration changes, and access to sensitive data stores

Risk appetite: the level of risk the organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives before action is deemed necessary

## 3 Policy Statements

### 3.1 Information Classification

All information assets shall be classified according to their sensitivity and criticality.
Classification levels are: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted. Asset owners are
responsible for classifying their information assets and applying appropriate controls.

### 3.2 Access Control

Access to information assets shall be granted on a least-privilege basis. All access rights
shall be reviewed at least annually. User accounts shall be disabled immediately upon
termination of employment or contract.

### 3.3 Acceptable Use

Company information assets shall be used for legitimate business purposes only. Personal
use is permitted within reasonable limits provided it does not interfere with work duties
or expose the company to legal or reputational risk.

### 3.4 Password and Authentication

All systems shall enforce strong authentication. Passwords must be a minimum of 12
characters and must not be shared. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all
remote access and privileged accounts.

### 3.5 Incident Reporting

All employees must report suspected information security incidents immediately to the
Security team at security@acme.com or via the internal ticketing system. Failure to
report a known incident may result in disciplinary action.

## 4 Risk Management

The organization shall conduct an annual information security risk assessment. Risk
treatment decisions shall be documented and approved by the Chief Information Security
Officer. Residual risks exceeding the risk appetite threshold require sign-off from the
Chief Executive Officer.

## 5 Compliance

### 5.1 Legal and Regulatory Requirements

The organization shall identify and comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, and
contractual requirements relating to information security, including data protection laws.

### 5.2 Audit and Review

This policy and its associated controls shall be reviewed annually or following a
significant security incident. Internal audits shall verify compliance with this policy.

## 6 Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination
of employment. Suspected criminal activity will be reported to relevant law enforcement
authorities.
